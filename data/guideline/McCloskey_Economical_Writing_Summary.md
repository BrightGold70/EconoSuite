# Economical Writing by Deirdre McCloskey
Key rules for writing in economics:

1. **Simple, Direct Sentences:** Avoid overly complicated phrasing. Clarity takes precedence over "academic" style.
2. **Cut Excess:** "Eschew surplusage." If a word can be removed without losing content, cut it.
3. **Active Verbs:** Use active rather than passive voice to bring vigor.
4. **Emphasis at the End:** Rearrange sentences so the most important information is placed at the end for maximum emphasis.
5. **The Implied Reader:** Write for a specific, intelligent audience (e.g., a colleague down the hall).

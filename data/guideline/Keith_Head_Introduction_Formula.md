# The Keith Head Introduction Formula
Adapted from Jim Brander by Keith Head.

1. **Hook:** Attract the reader's interest by explaining why the topic matters (puzzling, controversial, important for policy).
2. **Question:** Clearly state the research question. ("This paper addresses the question of...")
3. **Antecedents:** Briefly identify the critical prior literature and establish what is missing.
4. **Value-Added:** Clearly describe the main contributions of the paper relative to the existing literature.
5. **Road-map:** Provide a concise outline of the remainder of the paper.

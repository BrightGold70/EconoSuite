# International Trade Economics Terminology

1. **Intensive vs. Extensive Margins:**
   - *Intensive Margin:* Trading more volume/value of existing products with existing partners.
   - *Extensive Margin:* Trading new products or establishing relationships with new trading partners.

2. **Iceberg Transport Costs:**
   - Introduced by Paul Samuelson. The assumption that a fraction of a good "melts away" during transit. To deliver 1 unit, you must ship $T > 1$ units. This avoids modeling a separate transport sector.

3. **Gravity Model of Trade:**
   - Trade between two regions is proportional to their economic mass (GDP) and inversely proportional to friction (distance).
   - **Multilateral Resistance Terms:** Trade depends not just on bilateral barriers, but on barriers relative to the rest of the world (Anderson and van Wincoop 2003).

4. **Firm Heterogeneity:**
   - Melitz (2003) framework: Firms have different productivity levels. Only the most productive firms can overcome fixed costs to export.
